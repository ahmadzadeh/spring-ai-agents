# Supplier Risk Assessment Skill

## Scoring rubric (1–5 per dimension)

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Minimal risk – strong evidence, no concerns |
| 2 | Low risk – minor gaps, no significant concerns |
| 3 | Moderate risk – notable gaps or isolated concerns |
| 4 | High risk – multiple concerns or one critical issue |
| 5 | Critical risk – reject or mandatory escalation |

## RAG thresholds

- **Green**: score 1–2
- **Amber**: score 3
- **Red**: score 4–5

## Dimension-specific scoring guidance

### Operational Risk
- Score 5 if: single country dependency + no BCP documentation + recent supply disruptions
- Score 1 if: diversified operations, BCP in place, stable track record

### Compliance & Regulatory Risk
- Score 5 if: active regulatory investigation, expired/false certifications, GDPR violations
- Score 1 if: all required certifications verified, no regulatory issues found

### Reputational Risk
- Score 5 if: active major litigation, pattern of negative reviews, recent scandal
- Score 1 if: positive reviews, no adverse press, industry awards

### Cybersecurity Risk
- Score 5 if: recent data breach AND no ISO 27001 AND no responsible disclosure policy
- Score 1 if: ISO 27001 + SOC 2 verified, active responsible disclosure, no known incidents

### Financial Stability Risk
- Score 5 if: signs of insolvency, no public financial data, recent layoffs + investor exit
- Score 1 if: published audited accounts, investment grade signals, stable growth trajectory

## Mitigation action templates

For each Red/Amber finding, mitigation must be one of:
1. **Request documentation**: Ask supplier to provide evidence before proceeding
2. **Contractual condition**: Include specific clause in supplier contract
3. **Periodic audit**: Schedule review at 3, 6, or 12 months
4. **Escalate for manual review**: Flag for procurement leadership decision
5. **Reject**: Do not proceed with onboarding
